SOCIO-DEMOGRAPHIC ANALYSIS ABOUT P2P USERS IN ITALY

P2P technology is used everyday by millions of italians. But who are they? How do they behave on the internet? And moreover, where is Italy compared to other european countries? Let's start from the beginning:

WHAT IS PEER TO PEER?

In computing peer-to-peer is an expression that refers to a logical architecture of computer network in which nodes are not hierarchical only in the form of client or stationary server, but in the form of peer nodes that can act both as client and attendant to the other terminal nodes of the network. Through this configuration, any node is able to start or complete a transaction. The classic example of P2P is the
file sharing network.

AGE AND EDUCATIONAL LEVEL

The graph shows the use of internet and P2P in Italy in 2012 based on the age of the interviewees. The upper curve represents those using internet and, among these, are also highlighted those who use P2P. It can be deduced there is a peak in usage among young people which then falls in the age groups that include non-digital natives. The use of P2P has a trend similar to the internet one but involves about a quarter of the italian population.

Considering only those who claim to use P2P, it was analyzed the level of education, from elementary school up to degrees and PhD. The graph shows that the educational level is not relevant for P2P users: there is a majority of those who have graduated high school and middle school but in all groups there are people with different education. So P2P can be considered an accessible tool for people of all ages and education.

The graph shows the percentage of Internet and P2P usage among the population of the different Italian regions. Considering the percentage of internet usage, regions can be divided into three main clusters: in the North and in the capital utilization is higher than the Italian average of 47%, the central regions and Sardinia are in line with the national average while the South is less computerized.

WHAT DO WOMEN AND MEN DOWNLOAD?

Among P2P users, there are significant differences between men and women: women are about 1/3 of those using p2p and are on average a few years younger than men. By analyzing the type of material downloaded from the network it results that men and women have different habits: with the same average of media downloaded (music, video, games...) men download a lot more software than women.

TOTAL POP. VS P2P ADDICTION

Starting from the amount of P2P users in the year 2013, Italy is compared with other European countries. In the graph you can see that Italy is in 3rd place after Spain and United Kingdom considering the amount of people using P2P. So Italy is in line with the other major European countries that have a similar population. From the graph it can also be deduced that it is not necessary that if a nation is populated the use of P2P is widespread (note Germany).

ALL VS YOUNG

Considering the total population claim to use the internet in 2013 in every european country, it's interesting to take a look at the percentage of P2P usage. In the first graph are reported all those who use the internet while in the second one only the age group 16-24. It is thus evident that the percentage changes to prove once again that often the digital natives are those that exploit more this technology.

In this graph Italy is no longer at the top position for amount of P2P users but it's mid-table in european ranking. Looking at the percentage of internet users, countries like Lithuania and Ireland are more "P2P addicted" than Italy. Conclusion

In relation to Europe the amount of users is similar to the biggest countries' one and the percentage of usage results in line with the european average behavior.
